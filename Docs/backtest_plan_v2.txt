Plan: Replace Simulated Backtesting with Real 6-Month-Interval History
======================================================================

Context
-------
The current Signal History card shows 6 signals spaced ~21 trading days apart, each
measuring a 30-day forward return. This is dense/overlapping short-term noise, not a
meaningful track record. The goal is real historical buy/sell ratings at 6-month
intervals over the past ~18 months, with each rating's performance measured to the next
rating date.

Constraints & Data Availability
--------------------------------
Yahoo Finance valid range strings: "1y", "2y", "5y" (no "3y" or "4y").

With "2y" data (~504 bars, newest-first) and step_days=126 (approx 6 months):
  k=0: signal_idx=126, future_idx=0   -> 6mo ago  -> today          OK
  k=1: signal_idx=252, future_idx=126 -> 12mo ago -> 6mo ago        OK
  k=2: signal_idx=378, future_idx=252 -> 18mo ago -> 12mo ago       OK
  k=3: signal_idx=504, price_data[504:] = empty   -> can't train    FAIL

Conclusion: "2y" data supports exactly 3 reliable signals covering 18 months.
A 4th signal at 24 months back requires "5y" (excessive). 3 signals is correct.
